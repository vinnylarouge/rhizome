> **Innovation can only reach what is one step away from what already exists — but each step opens new adjacent steps, so the frontier expands as you explore it.**

## When it fires
- You are looking for a breakthrough but every viable move recombines things already in reach.
- A new capability just landed and a wave of "suddenly possible" combinations is now one step away.
- A roadmap demands a leap that skips the intermediate states that make it reachable.

## Questions it forces
- What is genuinely one recombination step away from what I already have?
- Which adjacent move would most expand the set of future moves?

## What to do
Stop reaching for the distant goal directly; enumerate the adjacent possible and take the step that maximally widens the next frontier. Stock the building blocks (components, skills, primitives) so more doors are one step away. Let exploration compound.

## Failure mode
Pure adjacency is myopic — it can wander into local optima and never make the costly jump a competitor takes. Some real innovation requires leaving the adjacent possible behind deliberately.

## Out-of-domain example
A startup's next product is rarely a moonshot; it's the recombination of capabilities it already shipped. Each launch makes new launches reachable — which is why platforms compound.
