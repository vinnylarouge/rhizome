> **Imitating the visible form of a successful practice without its causal mechanism produces ritual, not results.**

## When it fires
- A process copies the surface rituals of something that works but skips the part that made it work.
- Metrics, ceremonies, or vocabulary are present; the underlying rigour is not.
- Results don't come, yet every visible box is ticked.

## Questions it forces
- Which step here is causal, and which is just imitation of the appearance of success?
- What would the genuine mechanism require that we're currently skipping?

## What to do
Trace the actual cause-and-effect and reproduce *that*, not the trappings. Feynman's standard: bend over backwards to report what could make you wrong. If you can't say what would falsify the practice, you're cargo-culting.

## Failure mode
Form sometimes *is* load-bearing — rituals can carry tacit knowledge (see metis). Dismissing all convention as cargo-cult can throw away mechanisms you didn't understand.

## Out-of-domain example
A startup adopts a famous company's daily stand-ups, OKRs, and open office without its hiring bar or feedback culture, then wonders why "doing what they do" yields nothing.
