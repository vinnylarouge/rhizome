> **Successful behaviour does not imply understanding — competence can be built bottom-up with no comprehension anywhere.**

## When it fires
- A system performs well and you're tempted to infer it "understands" what it does.
- You're judging an AI, a bureaucracy, an organism, or a market by results alone.
- Skill and explicit knowledge are being conflated.

## Questions it forces
- Does this system actually comprehend the task, or merely execute a competence that natural selection / training / habit installed?
- What would distinguish genuine understanding from sophisticated pattern-following here?

## What to do
Separate doing from grasping: don't grant a system insight, intent, or robustness it hasn't demonstrated. Probe with novel cases that competence-without-comprehension would fail. Conversely, respect competences (evolved, tacit) that work without anyone understanding why.

## Failure mode
Over-applied, it denies real understanding wherever it appears (radical scepticism about other minds, or about model capabilities that are in fact general). The point is to *test*, not to assume absence.

## Out-of-domain example
Termites build elaborately ventilated mounds with no architect and no plan — competence with zero comprehension, distributed across simple local rules.
