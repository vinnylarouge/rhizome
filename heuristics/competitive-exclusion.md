> **Two actors competing for the exact same scarce resource cannot coexist indefinitely — one drives the other out or they differentiate.**

## When it fires
- Two products, teams, or organisms are chasing identical resources with no real differentiation.
- A market is consolidating toward one winner and you can't articulate why both rivals should survive.
- You and a competitor have become near-identical and the fight is escalating.

## Questions it forces
- What resource are we both competing for, and is our overlap total or partial?
- Can I differentiate the niche, or am I betting on out-competing a near-twin head-on?

## What to do
Differentiate the niche or commit to winning the resource war decisively — half-measures lose. Find the axis (geography, segment, timing, capability) where overlap drops, and specialise there. If you must compete head-on, ensure a durable edge, because ties don't persist.

## Failure mode
Real niches are multi-dimensional; "the same resource" is often an illusion that hides coexistence-enabling differences. Forced consolidation can destroy useful diversity and resilience.

## Out-of-domain example
Two open-source libraries solving the identical problem with identical trade-offs rarely both thrive — one's community, docs, or momentum wins and the other withers, unless one carves out a distinct use case.
