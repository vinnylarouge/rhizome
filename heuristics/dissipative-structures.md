> **Some order exists only while energy or material flows through it — stop the throughput and the structure dissolves.**

## When it fires
- A coherent pattern (a team, a city, a market, a flame) persists only as long as it's fed.
- You're tempted to treat a living, throughput-sustained system as if it were a static object you can freeze and store.
- Cutting the flow "to save resources" and expecting the structure to remain.

## Questions it forces
- What flow is currently holding this structure together?
- If the throughput stopped, would this order persist or evaporate?

## What to do
Identify the sustaining flow and protect it before optimising the structure. To maintain far-from-equilibrium order, keep the gradient fed; to dismantle a resilient structure, cut its throughput rather than attacking its form. Design for continuous flow, not static stock.

## Failure mode
Treating throughput as free — dissipative structures are expensive to maintain and can collapse abruptly when the flow drops below a threshold. Not all order is dissipative; some persists at equilibrium and needs no feeding.

## Out-of-domain example
A vibrant open-source community is a dissipative structure: it holds its shape only while contributions, attention, and maintenance flow through it. Defund the maintainers and the elaborate order decays fast.
