> **Build a small, vivid toy case that moves your judgement where abstract argument can't reach.**

## When it fires
- A problem is too abstract to get traction on.
- Two positions feel equally plausible and you need something to break the tie.
- You want to expose a hidden assumption in someone's reasoning.

## Questions it forces
- What minimal concrete scenario isolates the variable in question?
- When I turn the knobs of this toy case, which intuition flips — and is that flip trustworthy or a trick of the framing?

## What to do
Construct the simplest scenario that isolates the disputed factor, then vary its parameters and watch where intuition moves. Crucially, check whether the pump is *honest*: a good one reveals structure, a bad one smuggles the conclusion in through a loaded detail.

## Failure mode
Intuition pumps can mislead as easily as illuminate — a vivid irrelevant detail can hijack judgement. Always inspect which "knob" is actually driving the intuition.

## Out-of-domain example
To reason about fairness, Rawls' "veil of ignorance" is an intuition pump: imagine choosing society's rules without knowing your place in it, and your sense of just allocation shifts.
