> **Organisms don't just adapt to their environment — they modify the environment that then selects them, closing a feedback loop.**

## When it fires
- An actor is reshaping the very conditions that determine who wins, not just competing within fixed rules.
- A platform, standard, or incumbent has bent the landscape so it suits them and disadvantages entrants.
- Past actions are constraining present options through the environment, not through the actor directly.

## Questions it forces
- Who here is changing the rules of the game versus merely playing it well?
- What environment am I building that will later select for or against me?

## What to do
Treat the environment as a move, not a given. Construct the niche that selects for your strengths — build the standard, the data flywheel, the dependency others must adopt. When fighting a niche constructor, attack the constructed environment, not their current position.

## Failure mode
The environment you engineer outlives your intent and selects against you later (lock-in to your own legacy). Constructed niches can collapse when the throughput that maintains them stops.

## Out-of-domain example
A company that open-sources a framework constructs a developer ecosystem that selects for its own cloud services — competitors must now adapt to a landscape the incumbent built.
