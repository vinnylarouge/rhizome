> **When the future is uncertain, prefer moves that keep many future choices open at low cost.**

## When it fires
- Uncertainty is high and committing now would foreclose options you might want.
- A cheap action gives access to a large, unknown upside.
- You're tempted to lock in for the sake of looking decisive.

## Questions it forces
- Does this choice open or close future doors?
- What's the cost of keeping this option alive versus the upside if it pays?

## What to do
Buy cheap options on good outcomes and avoid premature lock-in: stage commitments, run reversible experiments, keep exits available. You don't need to predict — you need positions that are convex to uncertainty (small fixed downside, large open upside).

## Failure mode
Optionality has carrying costs and can become chronic non-commitment: an agent who never burns an option never builds anything durable. At some point you must commit to compound.

## Out-of-domain example
Taking a generalist's foundational skills early keeps many careers reachable; hyper-specialising too soon is a high-conviction bet that the chosen niche survives.
