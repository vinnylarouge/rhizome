> **Only variety can absorb variety: a controller must command at least as many responses as the disturbances it faces.**

## When it fires
- A regulator, manager, or model keeps getting surprised by situations it can't handle.
- The environment's complexity is growing faster than your repertoire of responses.
- A simple control scheme is failing against a rich, adaptive opponent or environment.

## Questions it forces
- How many distinct states can the environment throw at me, and how many distinct responses do I have?
- Am I outmatched on variety — and should I add responses or *reduce* the variety I must handle?

## What to do
Match variety on either side: amplify your own (more sensors, more response options, delegation, learning) or attenuate the environment's (constrain inputs, standardise, buffer). A controller that can't do either will be overwhelmed.

## Failure mode
Counting "variety" precisely is usually impossible, so the law guides rather than calculates. Adding response variety also adds cost and internal complexity that can itself become unmanageable.

## Out-of-domain example
A customer-support team with three canned macros against thousands of distinct issues drowns; it must either expand its repertoire (training, escalation paths) or shrink the input variety (better product, gating forms).
