> **When a shared resource is free to take but costly to all, individually rational use destroys it collectively.**

## When it fires
- A resource is shared, finite, and unowned: a pasture, a fishery, shared compute, a team's goodwill.
- Each actor's private benefit exceeds their share of the collective cost.
- Everyone behaves "reasonably" and the resource still collapses.

## Questions it forces
- Who bears the cost of each individual's use, and who captures the benefit?
- What externality is unpriced here?

## What to do
Internalise the externality: define boundaries and access rights, price or ration use, or build governance so the cost of overuse falls on the user. Don't rely on appeals to conscience against a structural incentive — change the structure.

## Failure mode
Hardin overstated inevitability: real communities often *do* self-govern commons (Ostrom). Imposing top-down privatisation or state control can destroy working local arrangements.

## Out-of-domain example
A shared GPU cluster where everyone launches "just one more" job: utilisation hits 100%, queues explode, and the resource becomes useless to all — unless allocation rules are imposed.
